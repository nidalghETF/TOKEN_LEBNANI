The core legal challenge is two-fold:

  • The Trust Gap: Bridging Lebanese physical assets with the digital expectations of GCC investors.
  • The Jurisdictional Bridge: Harmonizing Lebanese property law with ADGM English Common Law and FSRA regulations.

We are seeking a legal partner who possesses deep expertise in both jurisdictions to architect the "Legal Wrapper" that makes this bridge secure and enforceable.

2. THE TRANSACTIONAL ARCHITECTURE

Our proposed model relies on a Multi-Layered Corporate Hierarchy to ensure risk isolation and legal clarity. We require advisory counsel to validate and structure the following:

Layer 1: The ADGM SPV (The Issuer)

  • Domiciled in ADGM (Al Maryah Island).
  • Acts as the owner of the token issuance rights.
  • Function: Holds the FSRA Category 4 (Arranging) License.

Layer 2: The Lebanese Property Co. (The Asset Owner)

  • A Lebanese Limited Liability Company (SARL) holding the title deed to the specific real estate asset.
  • The Legal Question: How do we ensure the token holder's rights in the ADGM SPV are legally enforceable against the Lebanese property title?

Layer 3: The Facilities Management (Operations)

An independent third-party contractor handling rent collection and maintenance, keeping the SPV "passive" for regulatory purposes.

3. REGULATORY PERIMETER (FSRA STRATEGY)

Token Lebnani is pursuing a capital-efficient regulatory pathway within the ADGM FSRA RegLab Sandbox. Our strategy is predicated on strict adherence to a specific regulatory category:

  • Target License: Category 4 (Arranging Deals in Investments).
  • Capital Efficiency: Utilizing a framework that minimizes capital requirements while maintaining full compliance (vs. heavy Custody/Exchange license burdens).
  • Liquidity Strategy: To ensure immediate liquidity without Exchange classification, we will utilize ADGM-licensed Partner MTFs for secondary trading. Legal counsel must ensure the Primary Issuance structure remains distinct from the Secondary Trading venue.
  • Non-Custodial Status: We operate under "Option A," meaning investors hold their own private keys. We do not take custody of client assets.

This is the specific area where YazConsult's expertise is critical. We need to solve the following legal equation:

"How does a digital token holder in ADGM prove ownership of a brick-and-mortar building in Beirut in a Lebanese court?"
