Executive Summary

Token Lebnani represents a pioneering cross-border real estate tokenization platform that bridges the regulatory safety of the Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM) with high-yield Lebanese real estate assets. Our business model is designed to unlock approximately $150 billion in trapped diaspora wealth while providing institutional-grade protection under English Common Law.

Core Value Proposition

We solve the critical liquidity problem for Lebanese real estate by creating a regulated bridge between illiquid physical assets and the digital investment expectations of GCC investors, with immediate secondary market liquidity through ADGM-licensed MTFs.

Market Analysis

Target Market

Our primary market consists of Lebanese diaspora high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs) and professional investors based in GCC countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait). Secondary markets include non-Lebanese GCC investors seeking exposure to emerging market real estate with institutional protection.

Market Size

  • Total Addressable Market (TAM): $150+ billion in Lebanese diaspora wealth
  • Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): $25-30 billion in GCC-based Lebanese HNWI wealth seeking real estate exposure
  • Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM): $150-200 million in Year 1 capital deployment capacity

Competitive Landscape

The MENA real estate tokenization market is in its infancy. While UAE platforms like SmartCrowd and DIFX exist, none offer cross-border solutions for Lebanese assets with ADGM regulatory protection. Our first-mover advantage in this specific niche creates significant barriers to entry.

Business Model

Revenue Streams

  • Primary Issuance Fees: 3-5% of capital raised during token offerings
  • Asset Management Fees: 1-2% of Net Operating Income (NOI) collected quarterly
  • Future Revenue Streams: Listing fees for secondary trading (Year 2+), expansion into Egyptian market (Year 2+)

Cost Structure

  • Fixed Costs:
    • ADGM licensing and compliance: $35,000 annually
    • Team salaries (3 FTEs): $180,000 annually
    • Technology platform (Digishares): $48,000 annually
    • Legal and advisory retainers: $60,000 annually
  • Variable Costs:
    • SPV establishment fees: $5,000 per asset
    • Transaction legal fees: 0.5% of capital raised
    • Marketing and business development: 1% of capital raised

Unit Economics

Our "Net Zero Cost Model" for asset onboarding ensures that all setup costs are recovered from transaction fees, creating a self-sustaining operational model from the first transaction. For a typical $1 million asset:

  • Revenue: $30,000-$50,000 (issuance fee) + $10,000-$20,000 annually (management fee)
  • Costs: $5,000 (SPV setup) + $5,000 (legal) + $10,000 (marketing) = $20,000 one-time + $8,000 annually
  • Gross Margin: 60%+ on issuance, 60%+ on management fees

Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1: ADGM Sandbox (Year 1)

High-ticket concierge model targeting ultra-HNWIs with minimum investments of $25,000. Focus on relationship-driven sales through "Living Room" meetings and trusted advisor networks.

Phase 2: Market Expansion (Year 2)

Introduction of secondary market liquidity through ADGM MTF partnerships. Expansion into Egyptian real estate market with similar liquidity constraints. Gradual reduction of minimum investment thresholds.

Phase 3: Scale (Year 3+)

Full regulatory license activation. Expansion into SME equity tokenization. Development of proprietary technology stack reducing dependency on third-party platforms.

Regulatory Strategy

ADGM Sandbox Entry

Application for Category 4 "Arranging Deals in Investments" license within the FSRA Regulatory Laboratory (RegLab) framework. This capital-efficient approach requires approximately $50,000 in base capital versus $4M+ for custody licenses.

Compliance Framework

  • AML/CFT: Partnership with Waystone for outsourced MLRO function with internal oversight
  • Investor Protection: Non-custodial model (Option A) where investors retain control of private keys
  • Secondary Trading: Structured through partnerships with ADGM-licensed MTFs to avoid exchange classification

Legal Structure

Multi-layer corporate hierarchy with ADGM holding company owning Lebanese SPVs, with "look-through" provisions in Articles of Association recognizing token ledger as definitive shareholder register.

Risk Management

  • Legal/Regulatory Risk: Mitigated through YazConsult's cross-border legal opinion and ADGM regulatory framework
  • Operational Risk: Mitigated through independent facilities management companies and ring-fenced SPVs
  • Technology Risk: Mitigated through Digishares' institutional-grade platform and third-party smart contract audits
  • Market Risk: Mitigated through immediate secondary liquidity via MTF partnerships
